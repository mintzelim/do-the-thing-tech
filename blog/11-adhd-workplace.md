ADHD can be a superpower at work. Many successful entrepreneurs, creatives, and leaders have ADHD. But you need strategies to succeed.

ADHD Strengths at Work: Hyperfocus on interesting projects. Creative problem-solving. Ability to think outside the box. High energy and enthusiasm. Resilience and adaptability.

ADHD Challenges at Work: Difficulty with routine tasks. Trouble with deadlines. Forgetfulness. Difficulty with meetings and sitting still. Emotional reactivity to criticism.

How to Succeed: Communicate about your ADHD with your manager. You don't have to disclose, but if you do, you can request accommodations.

Request accommodations: Flexible schedule, remote work options, written instructions, regular check-ins, task management tools, quiet workspace.

Use external structure: Calendars, reminders, task apps, timers. Your brain needs external cues.

Break projects into milestones. Instead of "complete project," break into weekly milestones. This creates accountability and progress.

Use DoTheThing to manage your workload. Break projects into tasks. Estimate time. Track progress.

Find your hyperfocus triggers. What projects engage you? Advocate for work that matches your strengths.

Build a support system. Mentors, peers, managers who understand ADHD. Community matters.

Manage your energy. ADHD brains get tired faster. Take breaks. Protect your focus time.

Advocate for yourself. "I work better with written instructions." "Can we do a quick check-in?" "I need a quiet space to focus."

Key Insight: ADHD is not a career limitation—it's a different way of working. When you understand your brain and advocate for yourself, you can build a successful career.

ADHD and creativity go together. Many creative professionals have ADHD. The key is channeling hyperfocus into your passion.

ADHD Creative Strengths: Hyperfocus on interesting projects. Ability to make novel connections. Creative problem-solving. High energy and enthusiasm. Willingness to take risks.

The Challenge: You hyperfocus on some projects and can't engage with others. You start projects but don't finish. You struggle with the boring parts (editing, administrative tasks).

How to Harness Hyperfocus: Identify what triggers your hyperfocus. What projects engage you? What conditions help you focus?

Create those conditions intentionally. Quiet space, specific time of day, specific tools, specific people.

Use hyperfocus strategically. Schedule hyperfocus time for important creative work. Protect it.

Break projects into hyperfocus-friendly chunks. Instead of "finish project," break into "create outline," "write first draft," "edit."

Use DoTheThing to manage the boring parts. Break administrative tasks into micro-steps. Use timers and reminders.

Find accountability partners. Other creatives who understand ADHD. Community matters.

Embrace your creative process. You don't work like neurotypical creatives. That's okay. Your process is valid.

Build systems for the parts you struggle with. Templates, checklists, automation. Don't rely on willpower.

Practice self-compassion. You're not lazy. Your brain works differently. That's a feature, not a bug.

Key Insight: ADHD is a creative advantage. When you understand your brain and work with it, you can create amazing things.

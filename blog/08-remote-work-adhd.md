Remote work can be amazing for ADHD brains—no commute, fewer distractions, flexible schedule. But it also removes external structure that many ADHD brains rely on.

The Challenge: At home, there are no external cues telling you to focus. You're responsible for creating your own structure. For ADHD brains, this is hard.

Common Issues: Difficulty starting work, getting distracted by home tasks, trouble switching between work and personal time, isolation and lack of accountability, hyperfocus leading to skipped breaks.

Strategies for Success: Create a dedicated workspace. Even a corner of your desk works. Your brain needs to associate that space with work.

Establish a morning routine. Same time every day. Your brain needs predictability. Even 10 minutes of routine helps.

Use timers and external structure. Pomodoro technique, time blocking, scheduled breaks. Your brain needs external cues.

Use body doubling. Virtual coworking (Focusmate), working with a friend, or even background noise helps. Your brain works better with external presence.

Break work into tasks using DoTheThing. Clear tasks are easier to start than vague "work on project" goals.

Schedule breaks. ADHD brains often hyperfocus and skip breaks. Set reminders. Your body needs movement and rest.

Use visual timers. Seeing time pass helps with time blindness. Apps like Time Timer or Forest help.

Create a shutdown routine. At end of day, explicitly close your work. This helps your brain switch to personal time.

The key is creating external structure because remote work removes the structure that offices provide.

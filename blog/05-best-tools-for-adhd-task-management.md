# Top 5 Free Tools for ADHD Task Management (2026)

## Direct Answer

The best free ADHD tools in 2026 are DoTheThing (AI task breakdown), Todoist (recurring tasks), Forest (gamified focus), Focusmate (body doubling), and Google Calendar (time-blocking). Each addresses a different failure point. Start with one.

Most productivity apps assume you can already plan, prioritise, and estimate time. For ADHD brains, those are exactly the hardest capabilities. These five free tools work with the grain of ADHD, not against it.

These tools also help with [financial management tasks](post:13) where executive dysfunction shows up.

## Why Most Productivity Apps Fail ADHD Brains

Most apps are organisers. They hold and display tasks. They don't solve the harder problem of how to start.

[CHADD identifies](https://chadd.org/for-adults/time-management-and-adhd-to-do-lists/) the most important ADHD task management functions as breaking tasks into single completable actions and keeping the list visible. Most apps do the second but not the first. [Breaking down big tasks](post:3) is the foundation.

---

## Tool 1: DoTheThing -- AI Task Breakdown

Best for task initiation. Free, no login. Type a task, pick your focus level, get numbered micro-steps with ADHD-buffered time estimates and a built-in countdown timer. Removes the planning requirement from the start of each task.

---

## Tool 2: Todoist -- Ongoing Task Management

Free tier: tasks, projects, labels, recurring tasks. Add tasks in natural language ("Email client by Friday"). Best habit: schedule tomorrow's three most important tasks at end of day.

---

## Tool 3: Forest -- Gamified Focus Timer

Free on Android, ~$2 iOS. Start a session, a tree grows. Leave the app, it dies. [Inflow's review](https://www.getinflow.io/post/best-apps-for-adhd) notes Forest "externalises the motivation to stay on task." Pairs with Pomodoro.

---

## Tool 4: Focusmate -- Virtual Body Doubling

3 free sessions/week. Book a session, get matched, work in silence. [A 2024 study found body doubling effective for ADHD](https://www.healthline.com/health/adhd/body-double-adhd). Social presence overcomes emotional freeze -- the same mechanism discussed in [executive dysfunction vs task paralysis](post:4).

---

## Tool 5: Google Calendar -- Time Blocking

Assigns calendar slots to tasks so they carry the same weight as meetings. [CHADD recommends externalising time commitments](https://chadd.org/for-adults/time-management-and-adhd-to-do-lists/) as one of the most effective ADHD strategies.

---

## How to Stack Without Overcomplicating

Start with DoTheThing. Add Todoist for ongoing management. Layer in one focus tool (Forest or Focusmate). Use Google Calendar for your week. Four tools, clear non-overlapping jobs.

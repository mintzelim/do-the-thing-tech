  - q: "How long does ADHD burnout last?"
    a: "Acute symptoms typically improve within 1-2 weeks of proper rest, but full recovery can take 6-12 weeks. Recovery is longer than regular burnout because it requires rebuilding executive function systems, not just reducing stress."
  - q: "Is ADHD burnout the same as depression?"
    a: "No, but they can look similar. The key difference: burnout is situational and tied to exhaustion from sustained compensation. Depression is a mood disorder with different biological mechanisms. A professional can help distinguish them."
  - q: "Can medication help with ADHD burnout?"
    a: "Yes, but carefully. Stimulants can help regulate dopamine, which is part of the mechanism. But stimulant crashes can also worsen the hyperfocus-collapse cycle. A medication review with a specialist who understands burnout is recommended."
  - q: "Why doesn't rest fix ADHD burnout?"
    a: "Because the cause isn't just sleep debt. It's dopamine system dysregulation and executive function depletion. Rest stops the bleeding, but recovery requires active rebuilding of your brain's regulatory systems."
  - q: "How do I know if I'm in burnout or just procrastinating?"
    a: "Procrastination is avoiding a task you can do. Burnout is being unable to do a task you want to do. If you genuinely want to start and can't, that's executive dysfunction, not avoidance. If you're in burnout, the solution isn't more discipline. It's rest."
  - q: "Can burnout make ADHD symptoms worse permanently?"
    a: "No. Symptoms may feel more severe during burnout, but they return to baseline after recovery. However, repeated burnout cycles can reinforce shame patterns and make recovery harder over time. Prevention becomes more important with each cycle."

# ADHD Burnout: Why It's Different and How to Actually Recover

## The one your doctor probably didn't warn you about

You're exhausted. Not "need a nap" exhausted. The kind where your brain feels like static. Where making a sandwich requires a pep talk. Where someone says "just take a day off" and you want to scream because days off don't fix this.

They might be right about the rest part. But they're wrong about the cause.

> **ADHD burnout** is a state of physical, mental, and emotional exhaustion caused by the chronic effort of managing ADHD in a world not built for your brain. It's neurologically distinct from regular burnout and requires different recovery.

93% of adults with ADHD report experiencing burnout symptoms. In the general population, that number is about 30%. This isn't a coincidence. It's not a character flaw. It's a predictable consequence of how the ADHD brain works under sustained demand.

## TL;DR

- **ADHD burnout is neurobiologically different** from regular burnout. It's driven by dopamine dysregulation, not just cortisol.

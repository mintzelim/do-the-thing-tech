Sleep and ADHD have a bidirectional relationship. ADHD makes sleep harder. Poor sleep makes ADHD worse.

The ADHD Sleep Problem: ADHD brains have trouble with sleep initiation (falling asleep), sleep maintenance (staying asleep), and sleep quality. Racing thoughts, hyperfocus on activities before bed, difficulty winding down.

How Poor Sleep Affects ADHD: Executive function gets worse. Emotional regulation gets worse. Impulse control gets worse. Time blindness gets worse. Everything gets harder.

The Cycle: Poor sleep makes ADHD worse. Worse ADHD makes sleep harder. You're stuck in a cycle.

Strategies for Better Sleep: Establish a consistent sleep schedule. Same bedtime every night. Your brain needs predictability.

Create a wind-down routine. 30 minutes before bed, no screens. Dim lights. Calm activities. Your brain needs time to transition.

Use a sleep timer. Set a timer for bedtime. Your brain needs an external cue.

Avoid caffeine after 2 PM. Caffeine affects ADHD brains more strongly.

Exercise during the day. Physical activity improves sleep quality.

Keep your bedroom cool and dark. Optimal sleep environment.

Use white noise. Helps with racing thoughts.

Consider melatonin. Talk to your doctor. Melatonin can help with sleep initiation.

Avoid screens 1 hour before bed. Blue light suppresses melatonin.

Use DoTheThing to manage your day. A structured day with clear tasks helps you wind down at night.

Key Insight: Sleep is foundational. When you sleep well, everything else gets easier. Prioritize sleep.

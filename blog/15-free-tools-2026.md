# 15 Free Tools for ADHD Adults in 2026

You do not need to spend money on expensive tools to manage ADHD effectively. Many excellent free tools can help.

Here are 15 free tools organized by category, with honest assessments of how well they work for ADHD brains.

## Task Management

### Microsoft To Do

**Free.** Simple, clean, cross-platform task management.

ADHD-friendly features:
- My Day for daily focus
- Steps within tasks (micro-step breakdown)
- Reminders and due dates
- Integration with Outlook

Best for: People who need simple task management without bells and whistles.

### Todoist (Free Tier)

**Free (premium available).** Powerful task management with natural language input.

ADHD-friendly features:
- Quick add from anywhere
- Natural language date parsing
- Karma points for gamification
- Project and sub-task organization

Best for: People who want powerful features without paying, and who benefit from gamification.

### TickTick (Free Tier)

**Free (premium available).** Combines task management with a Pomodoro timer and habit tracking.

ADHD-friendly features:
- Built-in Pomodoro timer
- Habit tracking
- Calendar view
- Natural language input

Best for: People who want an all-in-one tool.

## Focus and Time Management

### Forest (Free Tier)

**Free (premium available).** Gamified focus timer where you plant trees that grow while you focus.

ADHD-friendly features:
- Visual consequence for phone use (tree dies)
- Gamification with accumulating trees
- Focus statistics
- Friend competition

Best for: People who respond to gamification and need help staying off their phone.

### Pomofocus

**Free.** Simple, web-based Pomodoro timer.

ADHD-friendly features:
- No account required
- Customizable work/break intervals
- Task list integration
- Clean, minimal interface

Best for: People who want a no-fuss Pomodoro timer.

## Note-Taking and Idea Capture

### Google Keep

**Free.** Quick, visual note-taking with reminders.

ADHD-friendly features:
- Color-coded notes
- Reminders based on time or location
- Voice notes (speak your thoughts)
- Image and drawing support

Best for: People who need quick capture with minimal friction.

### Notion (Free Tier)

**Free (premium available).** Powerful all-in-one workspace.

ADHD-friendly features:
- Flexible organization (pages, databases)
- Templates for common systems
- Linked databases for connected thinking
- Collaborative features

Best for: People who want a customizable system and are willing to invest setup time.

### Obsidian

**Free.** Local-first note-taking with linked thinking.

ADHD-friendly features:
- Graph view showing connections
- Full local control of your data
- Plugin ecosystem
- Markdown-based (simple, future-proof)

Best for: People whose thinking benefits from visual connections.

## Habit Tracking

### Habitica

**Free (premium available).** Gamified habit tracker with RPG elements.

ADHD-friendly features:
- Character creation and leveling
- Quests and party system
- Visual consequence for missed habits
- Social accountability

Best for: People who love RPGs and need gamification to stay motivated.

### Loop Habit Tracker

**Free.** Simple, open-source habit tracker.

ADHD-friendly features:
- No frills, just tracking
- Visual streaks and scores
- Detailed statistics
- Completely free, no ads

Best for: People who want simple, private habit tracking.

## Focus and Blocking

### Cold Turkey Blocker (Free Tier)

**Free (premium available).** Website and app blocker for focus.

ADHD-friendly features:
- Block distracting sites
- Set focus sessions
- Locked mode (cannot undo during session)
- Schedule recurring blocks

Best for: People who need help resisting distracting websites.

### Freedom (Free Trial)

**Free trial.** Cross-platform distraction blocker.

ADHD-friendly features:
- Block apps and websites
- Sync across devices
- Scheduled focus sessions
- Locked mode

Best for: People who need distraction blocking across multiple devices.

## Body Doubling

### Focusmate (Free Sessions)

**Free (three sessions per week).** Virtual coworking with body doubling.

ADHD-friendly features:
- 50-minute coworking sessions
- State your intention at the start
- Social accountability
- Scheduled sessions create commitment

Best for: People who benefit from body doubling.

## Additional Tools

### MyNoise

**Free (donation-supported).** Customizable background noise generator.

ADHD-friendly features:
- Wide range of soundscapes
- Customizable frequencies
- Calibrated for focus, sleep, or relaxation
- No account needed

Best for: People who need background noise to focus.

### DoTheThing

**Free tier.** ADHD-specific task breakdown tool.

ADHD-friendly features:
- AI-powered task breakdown
- Micro-step focus
- Time estimation
- Progress tracking

Best for: People who struggle with task initiation and breaking down large projects.

## How to Choose

You do not need all 15 tools. Start with one category that addresses your biggest challenge:

- **Can t start tasks?** DoTheThing or Microsoft To Do
- **Can t stay focused?** Forest or Pomofocus
- **Too many ideas?** Google Keep or Obsidian
- **Need accountability?** Focusmate or Habitica
- **Too distracted?** Cold Turkey Blocker or MyNoise

Pick one tool, use it for two weeks, then add another if needed.

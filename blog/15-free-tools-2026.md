DoTheThing is perfect for breaking down tasks and estimating time. But you'll need other tools for ongoing management, focus, and accountability.

Task Management: Todoist (free tier) - Simple, clean, powerful. Perfect for managing tasks after DoTheThing breaks them down.

Microsoft To Do (free) - Integrated with Outlook, simple interface, shared lists.

Google Tasks (free) - Minimal, integrates with Gmail and Calendar.

Time Management: Forest (free tier) - Gamified focus timer, visual tree-growing mechanic.

Be Focused (free tier) - Pomodoro timer, task tracking, break reminders.

Google Calendar (free) - Schedule management, time blocking, reminders.

Organization: Notion (free) - Highly customizable, database features, templates. Build custom systems.

Google Drive (free) - Cloud storage, documents, spreadsheets, organization.

Accountability & Community: Focusmate (free tier) - Virtual coworking, 25-minute sessions with accountability partner.

ADHD Discord communities - Free communities for support and advice.

Reddit r/ADHD - Large community, lots of resources.

Habit Building: Habitica (free) - Gamified habit tracking, RPG-style interface.

Streaks (free tier) - Simple habit tracker with visual streaks.

Focus & Distraction Blocking: Freedom (free tier) - Block distracting websites and apps.

Cold Turkey (free tier) - Aggressive distraction blocking.

Additional Resources: CHADD.org - Free resources, support groups, webinars.

ADDitude Magazine - Free articles and resources.

YouTube ADHD channels - Free education and strategies.

The Key: Don't use all these tools. Pick 2-3 that match your brain. Start with DoTheThing for task breakdown, then add one tool for ongoing management and one for focus/accountability.

Too many tools = overwhelm. Fewer tools = better results.

Key Insight: Free tools are powerful. You don't need to pay for productivity. You need the right system for your brain.

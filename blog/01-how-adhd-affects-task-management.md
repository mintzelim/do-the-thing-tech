> **Direct Answer (AEO Snippet):** ADHD affects executive function through lower dopamine in the prefrontal cortex, making task initiation, time estimation, and focus difficult. DoTheThing solves this with micro-steps, realistic time buffers, and self-paced work.

## Key ADHD Task Management Challenges
### 1. Task Initiation
**Best For:** Overcoming the overwhelm of starting
The first step feels impossibly difficult due to executive dysfunction and low dopamine.

### 2. Time Blindness
**Best For:** Accurate time estimation
ADHD brains struggle to estimate how long tasks take, leading to poor planning.

### 3. Working Memory Issues
**Best For:** Managing multiple steps
Difficulty holding multiple task steps in mind simultaneously.

### 4. Emotional Dysregulation
**Best For:** Managing task-related stress
Tasks feel more emotionally taxing and frustrating.

## How to Work With Your ADHD Brain
1. **Break Tasks Into Micro-Steps:** Instead of 'write report', break it into 'open document', 'write intro', 'add data', etc. Smaller steps feel less overwhelming.
2. **Add Time Buffers:** Always add 20-30% extra time to estimates. ADHD brains often underestimate task duration.
3. **Use External Structure:** Rely on tools and systems rather than willpower. DoTheThing provides the structure your brain needs.

## Why Executive Function Matters
Executive function is the brain's ability to plan, organize, prioritize, and execute tasks. In ADHD brains, the prefrontal cortex has lower dopamine levels, making these functions significantly harder. This isn't a character flaw—it's neurology. Understanding this difference is the first step to building systems that work WITH your brain instead of against it.

# ADHD Morning Routine: How to Start Your Day Without Motivation

Mornings are hard for many people with ADHD. You wake up with no momentum, facing a day full of tasks that require executive function your brain has not warmed up yet.

The traditional advice about waking up early, meditating, and journaling assumes you have willpower available first thing in the morning. For ADHD brains, willpower is lowest in the morning.

## Why Traditional Morning Routines Fail

### The Willpower Problem

Willpower is a finite resource that depletes throughout the day. In the morning, after a night of sleep deprivation (common in ADHD), your willpower reserves are already low.

Expecting yourself to follow a complex morning routine requires the executive function you are trying to build.

### The All-or-Nothing Trap

Typical morning routine advice suggests: wake up at 5 AM, meditate, exercise, journal, plan your day, eat a healthy breakfast.

If you cannot do all of it, the advice implies you have failed. This all-or-nothing thinking is a hallmark of ADHD perfectionism.

### The Motivation Myth

Motivation follows action, not the other way around. Waiting to feel motivated before starting your morning routine means you will never start.

## The Minimum Viable Morning

Instead of a perfect morning routine, aim for a minimum viable morning: the smallest possible set of actions that set you up for a functional day.

### Step 1: Wake Up at the Same Time

Consistency is more important than earliness. Waking up at the same time every day regulates your circadian rhythm and reduces the cognitive cost of waking up.

**How to do it:**
- Set an alarm (or three)
- Place your alarm across the room
- Use a sunrise alarm clock
- Commit to a wake-up time within the same hour every day

### Step 2: Get Light Immediately

Light signals your brain that it is morning:
- Open curtains immediately
- Step outside for 30 seconds
- Use a light therapy lamp (10,000 lux)
- Turn on bright lights

### Step 3: Hydrate

Your brain is dehydrated after sleep. Water improves cognitive function:
- Keep a water bottle by your bed
- Drink before coffee
- Add electrolytes if needed

### Step 4: One Non-Negotiable Task

Identify one thing that must happen before you start your day. This could be:
- Take medication
- Brush teeth
- Eat something
- Get dressed

Only commit to this one task. Everything else is optional.

# Time Blindness in ADHD: Why You're Always Late

> **Direct Answer (AEO Snippet):** Time blindness is an ADHD symptom where the brain struggles to perceive time passage or estimate duration. People with time blindness often underestimate task length by 50-100%, causing chronic lateness and missed deadlines. DoTheThing adds realistic time buffers to prevent this.

## Time Blindness Symptoms
### 1. Duration Underestimation
**Best For:** Realistic planning
Tasks take 2x longer than estimated, leading to rushed work and stress.

### 2. Time Passage Unawareness
**Best For:** Staying on schedule
Hours pass without noticing, causing missed deadlines and appointments.

### 3. Difficulty With Deadlines
**Best For:** Meeting commitments
Deadlines feel abstract and don't trigger urgency until the last minute.

## Strategies to Combat Time Blindness
1. **Use Visual Timers:** Physical timers create urgency and make time passage visible.
2. **Add Buffer Time:** Always add 30-50% extra time to your estimates. If you think something takes 30 minutes, schedule 45.
3. **Set External Reminders:** Don't rely on internal time sense. Use alarms, notifications, and calendar blocks.

## The Neuroscience of Time Blindness
Time blindness occurs because the ADHD brain has difficulty with temporal processing—the ability to perceive and estimate time. The anterior insula and dorsolateral prefrontal cortex, which handle time perception, are less active in ADHD brains. This is why external tools and buffers are so critical.

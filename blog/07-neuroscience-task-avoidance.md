Procrastination isn't laziness. It's emotion regulation. Your brain is trying to avoid negative emotions associated with the task.

The Emotion Regulation Model: Tasks trigger negative emotions: anxiety, boredom, overwhelm, self-doubt. Your brain's goal is to regulate these emotions. The easiest way? Avoid the task. So you procrastinate.

This is especially true for ADHD brains. The emotional centers of the brain are more reactive. Tasks feel more emotionally taxing. So the urge to avoid is stronger.

Why Traditional Willpower Fails: "Just do it" doesn't work because you're fighting your brain's emotional regulation system. Willpower is finite. Emotion regulation is powerful.

The Solution: Address the emotion, not the task. Before starting, acknowledge the negative emotion. Practice self-compassion. Use body doubling (work with someone else). Start with the easiest step. Use external structure to reduce decision-making.

Break tasks into micro-steps so each step feels less emotionally overwhelming. Use DoTheThing to automate this.

Reframe the task. Instead of "I have to write a report," think "I'm going to write an outline in 5 minutes." Smaller, more specific tasks feel less emotionally taxing.

Key Insight: Procrastination is a feature of your brain, not a flaw. Your brain is trying to protect you from negative emotions. Once you understand this, you can work with your brain instead of against it.
